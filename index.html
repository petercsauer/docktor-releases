Floating widgets alongside your Dock.

Features

Live Dock previews

Hover any Dock icon to see real-time thumbnails of every open window for that app, rendered with ScreenCaptureKit. Click a thumbnail to jump straight to it.

Music with synced lyrics

Compact transport for Spotify and Apple Music. Open the drawer for synced lyrics, queue inspection, and source switching.

Calendar from EventKit

Your Apple Calendar events, read locally. No OAuth, no sign-in, no Google. docktor never writes to your calendar.

Stackable timers

Run multiple countdowns in a dock-side widget without burying the timers you need to keep an eye on.

System stats at a glance

Keep CPU, memory, and disk telemetry visible next to the Dock without opening Activity Monitor.

On-device by default

Calendar events, supported music playback metadata, and Dock previews stay local. Spotify (when you sign in) and Open-Meteo (when Weather is on) are the only opt-in service calls.

Hover any Dock icon

Live thumbnails of an app's open windows pop in the moment you hover its Dock tile. ScreenCaptureKit renders the previews; per-app metadata tells you which Space each window lives on. Click one to jump straight to it -- no Mission Control.

Music, with the lyrics drawer Apple Music never gave you

A compact transport sits next to your Dock. Open the drawer for synced lyrics, queue inspection, and one-tap switching between Spotify and Apple Music.

A timer that doesn't get buried

Stack countdowns in a dock-side widget so a 12-minute pomodoro and a 45-minute 'stop reading email' timer stay visible without crowding the rest of your workspace.

Your data, your machine

Calendar reads locally via EventKit -- no Google OAuth, no sign-in. Supported music playback metadata and Dock previews stay local. Only Spotify (when you sign in) and Open-Meteo (when Weather is on) make outbound calls. Both are opt-in.

Permissions, in plain English

docktor asks for the minimum required to do its job. Every prompt is explained here.

Accessibility

Detect Dock position and read window metadata so widgets can dock next to it.

Leaves your Mac: Nothing.

Screen Recording

Render the live thumbnails you see when hovering a Dock icon.

Leaves your Mac: Nothing. Captures stay in-process.

Calendar

Show your Apple Calendar events in the Calendar widget. Read-only via EventKit.

Leaves your Mac: Nothing. docktor never writes events and never sends them off-device.

Location (optional)

Power the Weather widget via Open-Meteo. Skip it -- the widget just won't show until you grant.

Leaves your Mac: Coordinates go to Open-Meteo only when the Weather widget is enabled.

Notifications (optional)

Deliver timer alerts when a countdown finishes.

Leaves your Mac: Nothing.

Automation for Music (optional)

Control Apple Music playback locally when the Music widget needs Apple Events.

Leaves your Mac: Nothing.

Local Network (optional)

Complete Spotify's loopback OAuth callback on 127.0.0.1 while you connect your account.

Leaves your Mac: Nothing beyond the local callback.

What's new in 0.9.0

The first public pre-release.

Live Dock previews

ScreenCaptureKit-rendered thumbnails for every open window when you hover a Dock app.

Music with synced lyrics

Lyrics drawer inside the Music widget for tracks playing on Spotify or Apple Music.

Settings overhaul

New sidebar-driven Settings window with General, Widgets, Layout, About, and per-widget configuration.

Goodbye Google Calendar

Calendar reads exclusively from Apple Calendar via EventKit. The OAuth flow, client config, and any leftover tokens are gone -- a one-shot Keychain purge runs on first launch.

Stackable timers

Multiple countdowns at once in a dock-side widget so they don't fight for space.

Beta focus

Clipboard and Window Layout remain in development and are hidden from the public beta while we keep polishing them.
